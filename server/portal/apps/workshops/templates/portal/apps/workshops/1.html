Location

The Hackathon is located at the Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC) in the Advanced Computing Building (ACB, Building 205). TACC is located on the J.J. Pickle Research Campus (PRC) of The University of Texas at Austin. For more information, please visit TACC's Contact page.

When you arrive at ACB, please use the main entrance, and check in with the receptionist.

Parking on the Pickle Research Campus

After entering the gated campus at the main entrance at 10100 Burnet Road, stop and inform the guard that you are a TACC visitor. To get to the Advanced Computing Building (ACB, Building 205), continue past the guard booth, and past the chain link gate to Exploration Way, then make a right. ACB is the second to last building on the right with the overhanging patios.

When you arrive at ACB, you may park in any open spot in the covered lot, or on the street. Parking passes can be obtained from one of the kiosks on the street for a cost of $4/day.

Security/Forms

All attendees will be required to present a government-issued photo ID when checking in for this event.

If you are not a US citizen and have not filled out Darpa Form 60 within the last 12 months, you are required to do so and follow the instructions below in order for your registration to be complete.

All attendees will be required to present a government-issued photo ID when checking in for this event.

DARPA Form 60

If you are not a US citizen it is required that you fill out a DARPA Form 60 and submit the form by January 12, 2018 at 5pm EST. To download the DARPA Form 60 please click on the below link, reference the top of the page under Resources, and click on the U.S. Permanent Resident and Foreign National Visit Request (Form 60) link to access the form. Please make sure to submit the DARPA Form 60 by the deadline provided.

http://www.darpa.mil/policy/visitor-information

Options for Submitting the DARPA Form 60:

Please email your completed form to Katie Rogel at krogel@belcan.com. It is recommended the document be password-protected and to provide the password in a separate email.

The completed DARPA Form 60 can also be emailed directly to DARPA security at SIDInternationalSecurity@darpa.mil via encrypted e-mail. If this option is chosen, please also copy Katie Rogel (krogel@belcan.com) on the email so it is noted that your form was submitted.

Below you will find the program/meeting details that are required on the bottom section of the DARPA Form 60.

DARPA Point of Contact: Jen Roberts, I2O
